3.6 AI interpretation / OpenAI (third parties, cross-border transfers)

For AI interpretation, information you enter or select in the App is transmitted to an AI provider (currently OpenAI). This typically includes:

  • User inputs: question, free-text notes; mood/stress ratings and optional note text; in relationship readings, names, birthdates and zodiac sign context you provide.
  • Reading data: drawn cards, card positions, spread type, language setting.
  • Technical metadata (provider-dependent): IP address, timestamps, headers, and potentially device/app context, insofar as the provider processes this for API operation.

The App currently communicates with the AI provider via direct HTTPS API calls from your device. We do not run a proprietary backend for AI requests. This generally means your content is not routed through our own servers; however, it is transmitted to the third party and processed there.

The purpose is the generation of a textual interpretation and its return to the App.

Processing by the third party is governed by its own terms and policies. We do not fully control whether and for how long the provider stores data. Please also consult the provider’s privacy information (e.g., OpenAI policies).

Important: Depending on your configuration, the App may require an API key for the AI provider. Where you use your own API key, you are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of the key, complying with the provider’s terms, and ensuring you have the rights and consents required to transmit any third-party data.

Please do not enter sensitive data. In particular, avoid information you consider highly sensitive (e.g., health data, diagnoses, intimate details, bank data, passwords, full names of third parties without their consent). The App is not designed for such information.

Relationship readings: the App can include names and birthdates you enter for two people as part of the prompt context sent to the AI provider. Do not enter third-party data unless you have a lawful basis and, where required, the person’s consent.

A significant portion of App data is stored locally on your device (e.g., reading history, daily cards, settings). This means:

  • At present, we operate no own backend server to store your readings or personal profiles.

Typical on-device limits (current implementation): up to ~100 saved readings, up to ~365 daily cards, and an “archive” snapshot that may keep only the most recent entries (e.g., last 7 daily cards). These limits are implemented for usability and storage hygiene and may change with updates.

Local storage can include, depending on use: your questions, card IDs/positions, mood/stress ratings and optional note text, AI responses returned to the App, timestamps, language choice, as well as subscription entitlement cache values (e.g., last verified timestamps and active product identifiers). You can generally remove this data by uninstalling the App or using available in-app reset/delete functions (if provided).
